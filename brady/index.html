Common Problems and Signs You Need Termite Control in Brady, TX

Recognizing the early signs of a termite infestation is crucial for minimizing damage and the cost of repairs. Many signs can be subtle, but knowing what to look for can make a significant difference in early detection. If you observe any of the following indicators, it's highly recommended to contact a professional termite control service like San Angelo Termite for a thorough inspection. Ignoring these signs can lead to extensive damage that is much more challenging and expensive to remedy in the long run. Early intervention is always the best course of action when dealing with suspected termite activity. We are here to help identify and address these issues before they escalate further.

Wood Damage

Termites feed on wood, and their activity can lead to noticeable damage. This damage often appears as hollowed-out sections within wooden structures. You might notice wood that sounds hollow when tapped, or you may see blistering or darkening of wooden surfaces. Subterranean termites, in particular, consume the interior of wood, leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint. This type of damage can often go unnoticed until the infestation is significant. Drywood termites, on the other hand, can create galleries within the wood, which can weaken the structure over time. Visible signs of wood damage are a clear indication that you have a termite problem and require immediate professional attention. Our experts are skilled at identifying the type of wood damage caused by different termite species, which helps us determine the most effective treatment strategy. We can assess the extent of the damage and provide recommendations for repairs, working in conjunction with qualified contractors if needed.

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build mud tubes made of soil and wood particles. These tubes serve as protective pathways that allow them to travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your home's wooden structure) while staying protected from the environment and predators. You'll typically find these mud tubes on foundation walls, crawl spaces, or along pipes. The presence of mud tubes is a strong indicator of a subterranean termite infestation and should be addressed without delay. These tubes can range in size and shape, but even small, pencil-sized tubes can signify active termite activity. We know exactly where to look for these tell-tale tubes and can differentiate them from other harmless soil accumulations. If you discover mud tubes, it's important not to disturb them; doing so can cause the termites to abandon the tube and create new ones in less visible locations, making detection and treatment more difficult. Allowing us to inspect the tubes and the surrounding area is the most effective approach.

Discarded Wings

Termite swarmers, which are winged reproductive termites, are often the first visible sign of a termite infestation. These swarmers emerge from a colony to reproduce and establish new colonies. They shed their wings shortly after swarming, and you may find piles of these discarded wings near windowsills, doors, or other light sources. Finding termite wings indoors is a strong indication that there is an active termite colony nearby, likely within your home's structure. Termite swarming typically occurs during certain times of the year, often after rain, but finding wings at any time can be a cause for concern. It's important to differentiate termite wings from ant wings, as they can appear similar to the untrained eye. Our professionals are experts at identifying termite wings and interpreting their presence as a sign of current or historical termite activity. Even if you only find a few wings, it warrants an investigation by a pest control professional.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites produce dry, pellet-like droppings called frass. As they tunnel through wood, they push this frass out of small kick-out holes. You may find small piles of frass near infested wood. The appearance of frass is a clear sign of a drywood termite infestation and requires prompt attention to prevent further damage. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. While often found in small piles, the presence of any frass should be a red flag. Knowing what to look for is key to early detection, and our technicians are trained to spot even subtle signs of frass accumulation. Identifying frass helps us confirm the presence of drywood termites and implement the appropriate treatment strategy for this specific species.

Hollow Sounding Wood

As termites consume the interior of wooden structures, they can leave behind a hollow shell. Tapping on wood that has been damaged by termites can produce a hollow sound, unlike the solid sound of healthy wood. If you tap on beams, walls, or furniture and hear a hollow resonance, it could be an indicator of termite activity within the wood. This is often a sign of significant internal damage that may not be visible from the outside. We use specialized tools and techniques to detect hollow areas within wood, confirming the presence of termite damage even when it's not outwardly apparent. This method is part of our comprehensive inspection process, ensuring that no area goes unchecked, and all potential termite activity is identified. Identifying hollow-sounding wood is a crucial step in assessing the extent of the infestation and determining the necessary treatment measures.

Clicking Sounds from Walls

While not as common a sign as mud tubes or wood damage, in some cases, a large termite infestation can produce faint clicking sounds emanating from within walls. These sounds are made by soldier termites banging their heads against the wood to signal danger to the rest of the colony. While it might be difficult to hear, persistent clicking sounds from inside your walls, especially in quiet moments, could be an indication of a significant termite presence. This particular sign often indicates a larger, more established colony is at work within your home's structure. If you suspect you're hearing such sounds, it's definitely time to get a professional inspection to confirm whether it's related to termite activity. Distinguishing these sounds from other house noises can be challenging, but our experienced technicians have encountered this phenomenon and know how to investigate it effectively. Hearing clicking sounds should prompt immediate action to prevent further structural damage.
